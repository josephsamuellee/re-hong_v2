Quality assurance, ISO 9001 practices & automotive QMS alignment

Re Hong operates a quality-driven manufacturing environment in Taoyuan, Taiwan: preventive controls, disciplined supplier collaboration, and measurable shop-floor feedback. Our management system follows ISO 9001 quality principles; where programs require it—especially automotive—production is structured to meet IATF 16949 expectations (commonly searched as ISO/TS 16949 for legacy supply-chain language).

Prevention first—not paperwork after the fact

Quality planning begins before cutters touch material: risk reviews, controlled process routes, and alignment with your approval criteria. That mindset matches how leading OEM and Tier suppliers evaluate Taiwan CNC machining partners—especially when tolerances are tight and traceability must survive audits.

Advanced quality planning (AQP / APQP-style)

Early in a program we anchor requirements using structured methods—including failure modes analysis (FMEA) thinking and pre-validation steps—so volume production inherits stable characteristics instead of surprises. Documentation stays proportional to your industry and contract: automotive lanes emphasize PPAP-style readiness; industrial lanes may emphasize capability studies and inspection sampling plans.

Systematic measurement & rapid correction loops

Automated measurement is not “nice to have” reporting—it is how we watch drift before it becomes scrap. Data feeds trend review so adjustments happen quickly, protecting both your timeline and your incoming inspection burden.
